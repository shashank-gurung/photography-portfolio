BLACK & WHITE CHRONICLES

I’m Shashank Gurung, a B.Tech student with a strong interest in photography and visual storytelling.

Alongside my studies, I spend time capturing everyday moments, people, and spaces that feel natural and unforced.

A Closer Look

The Silent Gaze

This is my favorite photograph because it captures a rare moment of absolute raw curiosity. I took this while observing how a child interacts with a world that seems massive and complex to them.

"There is something deep in the way she looks back—it's not just a photo of a child; it's a reminder of the innocence we lose as we grow up. The scratches on the car and the rough textures represent the world's chaos, while her expression remains the only calm element."

The Broken Wall

This image came from noticing a structure slowly giving in to time, shaped by cracks, rough edges, and layers worn down by years of exposure.

"What stays with me is the contrast between what remains and what has already disappeared. The solid surface still tries to hold its form, while the dark opening interrupts it completely, turning emptiness into the most expressive part of the frame."

The Empty Swing

This photograph came from noticing a playground without movement, where something meant for joy was left completely still.

"What drew me in was the sense of pause it created. The absence of people makes the space feel heavier, turning a familiar object into a quiet reminder of moments that have passed."

The Quiet Distance

This photograph came from noticing a moment that wasn’t meant to be seen as a subject. Two people sit close, yet their presence feels partially hidden.

"What stays with me is the balance between closeness and distance. The frame suggests how relationships often exist in small, unnoticed moments rather than obvious gestures."

The Waiting Cycle

This photograph came from noticing a moment where everything felt paused. A bicycle rests among empty chairs, surrounded by objects that suggest activity.

"What stays with me is the stillness it holds. The cycle feels ready to move, but remains grounded—a quiet reminder of routines and the unnoticed gaps between arrival and departure."
